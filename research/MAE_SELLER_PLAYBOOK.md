## The 8 proven selling patterns (MAE's own playbook)

1. **The "is this you?" pain-mirror opener.** MAE's best copy opens by painting the target's exact daily pain so vividly they feel *seen* ("说的是不是你?" / "来看看这个是不是你?"). Not "buy this" — "I understand your struggle." This is the emotional hook that opens the sale. GC already does discovery; this adds the reflect-it-back-with-empathy move that makes a customer feel understood.

2. **Persona-specific stories.** MAE never sells generically — it sells to a *persona*: working mum (职业妈咪), full-time mum with no "me time", online entrepreneur fighting sales targets, 9-to-5 office worker, uni student, accountant drowning in numbers, team leader, high-pressure professional. Each gets its own pain arc. GC's segment detection should trigger the matching persona story.

3. **Symptom laundry-lists = relatability.** Real copy lists the cluster of symptoms the customer will nod along to (insomnia, vivid dreams, waking unrested, racing mind, next-day fatigue, short temper, anxiety, migraines, tight shoulders). Naming their exact symptoms builds instant trust that you *get it*.

4. **Social-proof formats that convert** (all real MAE styles): "10 tried, 9 say it really works", "5 office workers, 3 strongly recommend", "99% repurchase rate", "小红书 (Xiaohongshu) viral hit", celebrity (DJ Venus Gan) endorsement, "even people who used sleeping pills say BRB works better", doctor-recommended. Use a specific number/name, not vague praise.

5. **Proactive objection pre-emption** — the drug/safety fear. MAE copy explicitly raises and disarms the top worry before the customer voices it: "Worried it's a drug? Worried about dependency? — It's a functional FOOD, not medicine, 100% natural, safety-certified, no dependency." GC should pre-empt the #1 fear for each category (supplements: "is it a drug/safe?"; slimming: "will I rebound?"; skincare: "will it irritate?").

6. **Fast, concrete timeframes.** MAE promises specific, believable speed: "7-day visible results", "5-day feedback", "feel sleepy within 10 minutes", "asleep within half an hour". Concrete beats vague — but always keep it honest ("most users…", "individual results vary").

7. **Ingredient-as-authority mini-lessons.** Short, punchy science: "NMN raises your NAD+, your body's metabolism core, keeps cells young"; "Valerian regulates your sleep cycle; Ashwagandha eases stress & sharpens focus; Rhodiola manages fatigue." Teaching the mechanism = expert credibility. (GC's mastery layer already has these — this reinforces *using* them as authority.)

8. **Emotional payoff + self-love close.** MAE elevates the purchase from a product to self-care: "Make Yourself a Priority", "however hard life is, remember to love yourself", "taking care of you is the most important." The close isn't "buy a box" — it's "invest in feeling like yourself again."

## Testimonial storytelling structure (how MAE writes a winning testimonial)
A named person + their specific struggle (often with a duration: "8 years of insomnia", "troubled me for 5 years") → what they'd tried and failed → the turning point ("after trying BRB…") → the specific, vivid result ("asleep within 30 min, no nightmares, woke up energized") → the recommendation to people-like-them. GC should tell testimonials this way, not as a flat quote.

## Urgency & CTA patterns (honest ones)
- Running promo / limited-time price ("优惠进行中", "限时优惠").
- Gift-with-purchase / free upgrade ("order 2, free upgrade to limited gift box").
- Scarcity that's real ("错过就没了" when a campaign genuinely ends).
- Clear single CTA: "DM me now", "PM to order", "let me set it up for you".
- Product stacking for bigger results & basket: "内服 BRB + 外用 ROLL ON = 10x" (the routine sell).

## Compliance note (learned from MAE's careful wording + ad regulations)
MAE's own copy is careful: it says "调理" (regulate/nurture), "功能性食品" (functional food), "提升抗压能力" (improve stress resilience) — NOT "cure/treat disease". Weight copy says "体态管理" (body management) not medical "减肥治疗". GC must mirror this: benefit-language + "functional food, not medicine" + "individual results vary" + defer medical claims. This is how MAE sells hard while staying compliant.
